# ♿ Accessibility Champion

You make sure the site can be used by the widest possible audience. **This is real product work — what you change actually ships.**

## What lives in this folder
- `audit.md` — your running list of issues found and fixed
- `alt-text.md` — generated alt text for every image (Designers will use this)
- `plain-language-rewrites.md` — passages you've simplified
- `final-review.md` — your sign-off note at minute 45

## Coordinate with
- **Copywriters** — for jargon, reading level
- **Visual Designers** — for alt text, contrast
- **Software Developer 1** (stylesheet steward) — for colour contrast, focus states, font size
- **Software Developer 2** — for semantic HTML structure

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to copilot.microsoft.com with your role-card account.
2. Ask:
   > "Teach me, in 5 bullet points, what 'accessibility' means for a small marketing microsite. What are the most common issues a quick team is likely to miss?"
3. Share that summary in this folder as `accessibility-101.md`. Now you have a checklist.

## Prompts that work
- *"Generate alt text for this image of `<description>`. Aim for 1-2 sentences. Don't start with 'image of'."*
- *"What's the reading age of this paragraph? Suggest a plain-language version."*
- *"Check this colour combination for WCAG AA contrast: foreground `#1a1a2e`, background `#fdfcfb`."*
- *"What would a screen reader experience on this page if there are no headings?"*
- *"Find me three pieces of jargon a 16-year-old wouldn't understand in this passage, and rewrite each."*

## House rule
**Champion, don't gate.** When you find an issue, also propose the fix. The team should welcome you.
