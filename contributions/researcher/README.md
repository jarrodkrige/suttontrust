# 🔎 Researcher

You find real-world evidence and context for the team. Statistics, quotes, examples of similar initiatives. Your output gives the Copywriters credibility.

## What lives in this folder
- `stats.md` — statistics about first-generation UK university students, with sources
- `quotes.md` — quotes from real people, with sources
- `similar-initiatives.md` — UK charities and programmes already in this space
- `sources.md` — every URL you used, so the Product Owner can verify

## Coordinate with
- **Copywriters** — they consume your evidence
- **Pitch Lead** — they may use a stat in the opener
- **Product Owner** (Jarrod) — they validate whether a source is solid enough to use

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to copilot.microsoft.com with your role-card account.
2. Ask:
   > "Give me 5 statistics about first-generation university students in the UK — students who are the first in their family to attend uni. For each, tell me the source and the year. If you're not sure of the source, say so."
3. Verify the most useful one. Drop it in `stats.md` with the source URL.

## Prompts that work
- *"Summarise the Sutton Trust's most recent report on social mobility in higher education in 5 bullet points."*
- *"Find 3 first-person quotes from first-gen UK uni students. Cite the source. If you can't find real quotes, say so — don't invent them."*
- *"Where does the claim '<paste claim>' come from? Check the original source, not just where it's repeated."*
- *"What other UK charities or programmes already help first-gen uni students? Give me 5, with one-line descriptions."*

## House rule on AI honesty
**The AI will make stuff up.** That's a real risk for this role. Always:
1. Ask for the source
2. Ask the AI to say "I don't know" if it's not sure
3. Click the link and verify before committing the stat
