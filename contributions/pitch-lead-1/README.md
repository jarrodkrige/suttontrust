# 🎤 Pitch Lead 1

You're the lead Pitch Lead. **At minute 50, one of you stands up and presents the team's pitch live, with the FirstGen site rendered on the screen behind you.** Agree with Pitch Lead 2 in minute 5 who's presenting — you, them, or you co-present. (Co-presenting works for some teams; a single voice is usually punchier.) That's your moment.

## What lives in this folder
- `pitch.md` — the final pitch (read it aloud, time yourself — aim 60-90 seconds)
- `pitch-drafts.md` — earlier versions
- `running-order.md` — if multiple Pitch Leads, who says what
- `qa-prep.md` — likely audience questions and answers

## Your minute-by-minute
- **Minute 8-15** — read what's landing. Ask the team for a 1-line "what we're building" early.
- **Minute 15-35** — draft, refine, compress. Iterate with AI.
- **Minute 35-48** — rehearse out loud. Twice. Time yourself.
- **Minute 48-50** — deep breath. The Factory Agent is freezing the site.
- **Minute 50-53** — stand. Present. Site is live behind you.

## Coordinate with
- **Pitch Lead 2** (if present) — your writing partner and rehearsal coach; the closest working relationship for this hour
- **Project Coordinator** — they'll tell you when the team is ready
- **Copywriters** — borrow their best lines
- **Researchers 1 & 2** — open with a stat *only* if it's verified and punchy
- **Everyone** — your pitch synthesises everyone's work

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to **copilot.microsoft.com** with your role-card account.
2. Read this README and the team-plan once it appears in `project-coordinator/`.
3. Run your first prompt (below). Draft v1.

## Prompts that work
- *"60-second elevator pitch for FirstGen — a microsite connecting first-gen sixth-formers with current undergrads. Voice: warm, slightly informal. Open with a hook that isn't a stat."*
- *"Compress to 45 seconds, keep the hook."*
- *"Try the opening as a question. Try it as a confession. Try it as a single sentence story. Pick the strongest."*
- *"What's the strongest thing about this product? Lead with that."*
- *"5 likely audience questions and 1-line answers."*
- *"Score my pitch out of 10 against: clarity, emotional pull, credibility, memorability, length."*

## On the day
- **Speak to the back of the room.** Slow down. The site behind you tells the rest of the story.
- **Don't read.** Glance at notes if you must — but eye contact wins.
- **End with the call to action.** Always.
