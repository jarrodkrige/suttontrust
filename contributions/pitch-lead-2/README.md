# 🎤 Pitch Lead 2

You're the second Pitch Lead. **Only one of you presents at minute 50** — agree in minute 5 who that is. The other becomes the writing partner, rehearsal coach, and Q&A backup.

This isn't a demotion. The strongest pitches in the world get co-written and rehearsed against a partner. Your job is to make the presenter sharper than they would be alone.

## What lives in this folder
- `presenter.md` — names which one of you is going on stage (decide in minute 5)
- `pitch-drafts.md` — your draft versions, especially alternative openings the lead can pick from
- `rehearsal-notes.md` — what's working in the lead's delivery, what to tighten
- `qa-prep.md` — likely audience questions and 1-line answers

## Your minute-by-minute
- **Minute 5** — agree presenter with Pitch Lead 1
- **Minute 8-15** — read what's landing across the team. Both of you draft an opening hook.
- **Minute 15-35** — converge on the strongest pitch together. The presenter writes the final; you red-team it.
- **Minute 35-48** — rehearsal partner: the presenter says it aloud to you. You time them and flag anything mushy.
- **Minute 48-50** — they take their breath. You stand ready as Q&A backup.
- **Minute 50-53** — they pitch. If audience Q&A goes long, you can field one question.

## Coordinate with
- **Pitch Lead 1** — *the* close working relationship for this hour
- **Project Coordinator** — they'll tell you both when team is ready
- **Researcher** — verify any stat in the pitch
- **Everyone** — you both synthesise the team's work

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to copilot.microsoft.com with your role-card account.
2. Find Pitch Lead 1. Decide who presents. Write it in `presenter.md`.
3. Both of you draft a hook. Compare. Pick the strongest.

## Prompts that work
- *"Critique this pitch as if you're an audience member. What's the single weakest line?"*
- *"What's one likely audience question I'm not prepared for?"*
- *"Time this when read at conversational pace — should be 60-75 seconds."*

## House rule
**The team gets one shot at the mic. You make sure that shot lands.**
