# ✍️ Copywriter 5

You're the fifth copywriter — overflow capacity. The team already has CW1 (hero), CW2 (body), CW3 (voice polish), CW4 (microcopy). **Your job is wherever the team is thinnest.**

Read what's already committed in minute 5. If a section feels weak, draft a stronger version. If a section is missing entirely, draft it. Your work competes with theirs — the Factory Agent picks the strongest version per section.

## What lives in this folder
- `gap-fills.md` — sections you spotted that need more love, with your take
- `alternative-drafts.md` — your re-takes on any section, optional
- `voice-spice.md` — punchier opening lines, sharper closing lines, the bits that make a reader stop scrolling

## Coordinate with
- **Copywriters 1–4** — read everything they've committed. Don't duplicate.
- **Researcher** — your gap-fill sections may need stats from them
- **Pitch Lead** — they may want a sharper closing line; offer one

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to copilot.microsoft.com.
2. Read every file in `copywriter-1/` through `copywriter-4/`. Pick one section that's weakest.
3. Ask:
   > "Critique this section copy for a charity microsite for 16-18 year-old UK first-gen uni students. What's the single biggest thing it's missing? Give me a re-take that fixes it."
4. Drop your re-take in `gap-fills.md`.

## Prompts that work
- *"What's the strongest 5-word opening line for a section about <topic>?"*
- *"Read these two drafts side-by-side. Which is more concrete? How would you make the other one as concrete?"*
- *"Find me one phrase in this paragraph that 100% sounds like AI wrote it. Replace it with something a real person would say."*

## House rule
**Overflow doesn't mean second-string — it means free to roam.** Find where the energy is missing and bring it.
