# 🔎 Researcher 2

You're the second researcher. Researcher-1 owns the headline stats and the trust-list of sources. **Your job is the human story**: real quotes, real anecdotes, real student voices that the Copywriters can weave in.

A page full of numbers is cold. A page with one believable quote and a real number is moving. You bring the quote.

## What lives in this folder
- `quotes.md` — quotes from real first-gen UK students, with sources (interview transcripts, blog posts, charity reports — never invented)
- `personas.md` — short profiles (3-4 lines) of typical first-gen sixth-formers — to inform copy choices, not to publish
- `cross-checks.md` — where you double-checked Researcher-1's stats against a second source
- `sources.md` — every URL you used

## Coordinate with
- **Researcher 1** — divide the workload at minute 5: they own stats, you own quotes (or split differently — agree)
- **Copywriters** — they consume your quotes
- **Pitch Lead** — they may want one quote in the pitch
- **Visual Designer 2** — they may want to caption a portrait with one of your quotes

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to copilot.microsoft.com.
2. Find Researcher-1. Agree the split.
3. Ask:
   > "Find 3 first-person quotes from UK first-generation university students — students who were the first in their family to attend uni. Give the source URL for each. If you can't find one, say so. Do not invent quotes."
4. Verify the most useful one — click the source link before committing.

## Prompts that work
- *"Summarise the Sutton Trust's most recent report on first-gen students in 5 bullet points. Include URL."*
- *"Find a real story (blog post, interview, news article) of a first-gen UK student's first week at university. Give source."*
- *"Cross-check this stat — '<paste>' — does the original source say what we're claiming?"*

## House rule on AI honesty
**The AI will make stuff up — especially quotes.** If a quote sounds too perfect, it probably is. **Always verify with a click before committing.** When in doubt, ask the AI to tell you it doesn't know.
