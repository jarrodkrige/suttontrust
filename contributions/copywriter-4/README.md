# ✍️ Copywriter 4

You're the fourth copywriter — extra capacity for microcopy and conversion. CW1 owns the hero; CW2 owns the body sections; CW3 polishes voice. **Your job is the small words that do big work**: button labels, nav text, error states, footer line, the closing-CTA microcopy.

People underestimate this layer — it's where the site stops feeling like a brochure and starts feeling like a tool.

## What lives in this folder
- `microcopy.md` — every short string the site needs (nav items, button labels, footer, ARIA labels, empty states)
- `cta-variants.md` — multiple takes on the closing CTA in case the Pitch Lead wants to A/B
- `signup-flow-words.md` — what the "Join" section actually says (joins what? signs up to what? for whom?)

## Coordinate with
- **Copywriters 1–3** — keep your microcopy in their voice
- **Pitch Lead** — they own the headline CTA; you own the supporting microcopy around it
- **Software Developers** — flag where you need text (e.g. *"button label for the form submit"*)
- **Accessibility Champion** — they'll review your ARIA labels for clarity

## First 2 minutes
1. Sign in to copilot.microsoft.com.
2. Read what CW1, CW2, and the Pitch Lead are landing on for tone.
3. Ask:
   > "I'm writing microcopy for a charity microsite for first-gen UK sixth-formers. Give me 5 button labels for a 'sign up to chat with a current uni student' CTA. Warm, action-oriented, no marketing speak."
4. Pick the best, add 4 more variants. Drop in `cta-variants.md`.

## Prompts that work
- *"Rewrite this nav item — 'About' — in 1-3 words that say what's actually behind the link."*
- *"What's a friendlier version of 'Submit' for a sign-up button on a charity site?"*
- *"Three honest, non-cheesy footer lines for FirstGen — a charity helping first-gen UK uni students."*

## House rule
**Microcopy isn't decoration.** Every label is a small promise. Make it specific and honest.
